About The Project

In Malawi, a low-income nation, there are challenges in obtaining non-destructive technologies for assessing the freshness of meat. Current methods, such as pH measurement and microbiological testing, are both inaccurate and costly, requiring specialized training. To address this issue, there is a pressing need for an affordable, user-friendly, and non-destructive system for evaluating meat freshness. This system would benefit both consumers and meat producers, aiding them in assessing meat quality in local markets and supermarkets. Meat quality encompasses various factors, including nutritional content, tenderness, and marbling, which go beyond the basic assessment of freshness.
